# 4. Why Are You Still an IC After 8 Years?

### Interview Ready Answer

This question is a trap. Never sound defensive.

**Answer:**

"I believe leadership is not only about having direct reports but also about influencing technical decisions and driving outcomes.

Over the years, I have focused on becoming technically strong in cloud, automation, Kubernetes, and DevOps practices. My role has required me to take ownership of critical platforms and mentor team members informally.

The opportunity for formal people management did not arise in my current organization, but I have consistently demonstrated leadership through technical ownership, collaboration, and mentoring.

Going forward, I am open to taking on team leadership responsibilities while continuing to contribute technically."

---

# 9. Difference Between NSG and WAF

### Interview Ready Answer

| NSG                              | WAF                          |
| -------------------------------- | ---------------------------- |
| Network Security                 | Application Security         |
| Layer 3 & Layer 4                | Layer 7                      |
| Controls IPs and Ports           | Inspects HTTP/HTTPS Traffic  |
| Allows or Denies Network Traffic | Protects Against Web Attacks |
| Example: Allow Port 443          | Example: Block SQL Injection |

### Interview Summary

"NSG controls who can connect to a resource, while WAF inspects and protects the application traffic itself."

This is the most important leadership question.

**Answer:**

"First, I would focus on understanding the team's strengths, challenges, and responsibilities.

My approach would be:

* Set clear goals and expectations
* Encourage collaboration and knowledge sharing
* Promote automation and best practices

* Ensure high operational standards
